Skilled Worker visa

You can apply for a Skilled Worker visa if you have been offered a skilled job in the UK by a Home Office-licensed sponsor.

Eligibility

To qualify for a Skilled Worker visa, you must:

  • work for a UK employer that's been approved by the Home Office
  • have a 'certificate of sponsorship' from your employer
  • do a job that's on the list of eligible occupations
  • be paid a minimum salary - the amount is usually £38,700 per year, or the 'going rate' for your job, whichever is higher

How long it lasts

Your visa can last for up to 5 years before you need to extend it. After 5 years cumulative residence you may be eligible to apply for indefinite leave to remain.

Fees

Application fees range from £719 to £1,500 depending on length of stay and where you apply from. You'll also pay the Immigration Health Surcharge of £1,035 per year.

Knowledge of English

You must demonstrate English language ability at CEFR level B1 or above by passing an approved test, holding a degree taught in English, or being a national of a majority-English-speaking country.

Documents

You'll need a current passport, your certificate of sponsorship reference number, proof of knowledge of English, and a tuberculosis test result if from certain countries.

Apply from outside the UK

Decisions usually take up to 3 weeks once you've attended your biometric appointment.
